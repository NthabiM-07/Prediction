# Transcript 2: INGB413 Report Strategy Guidance

The lecturer’s voice memo is mainly giving **strategy for how to write the INGB413 GA report**, not only how to calculate. The key message is that the report must tell a **validated engineering investigation story** from problem context, literature, data, analysis, interpretation, and conclusion.

## What the lecturer is saying in clear terms

Your report must not only show charts and calculations. It must explain **why each method was chosen**, how it applies to the Tangra Inc. problem, and what the results mean for the business.

This fits the 2026 assignment because it assesses **ECSA Graduate Attribute 4**, which requires research-based knowledge, design of experiments, analysis and interpretation of data, and synthesis of information to reach valid conclusions .

## Main points from the voice memo

### 1. Start with a literature-supported narrative

The lecturer says you should first open a Word document and begin by writing broadly about quality management. For example:

Quality management in organisations is often linked to variation, defects, customer satisfaction, process control and profitability.

Then you support this with sources. The lecturer specifically encourages using **Google Scholar, Scopus, textbooks and articles** to find literature on:

Quality management
Statistical Process Control
Process capability
Sampling inspection
Defects and defectives
Process variation
Cost of quality
Manufacturing quality

This links directly with Montgomery’s textbook, which explains that quality improvement is strongly linked to reducing variability and waste, and that statistical methods are central to quality improvement .

### 2. Use headings or “hashtag ideas” before writing

When the lecturer says “hashtags”, she means **mini-topic headings** or argument points. For example:

What is quality management?
Why is quality management important in manufacturing?
What is SPC?
Why is a p-chart suitable for Process 1?
Why is a g-chart suitable for Process 2?
What is DPMO?
What does 2-sigma mean?
What are the cost implications of defects?
Which process is more capable?
Which process should Tangra Inc. use?

The point is to build the report around a logical argument, not random calculations.

### 3. Literature can be integrated, not only placed in a separate literature review

The lecturer says you can either write a separate literature review section or integrate theory into the relevant analysis sections.

For your report, the best option is likely **both**:

Use a short literature review after the background, then also use literature inside the analysis when justifying each technique.

For example, before using a p-chart, briefly explain that p-charts are suitable for monitoring the proportion of nonconforming items in attribute data. This matches the logic used in last year’s stronger reports, where Process 1 was linked to p-chart analysis and Process 2 was linked to g-chart analysis .

### 4. Explain why the selected tools are correct

The lecturer stresses that marks come from the **application of the chosen technique**, not from merely inserting a chart.

For Process 1, you must explain that the process uses fixed samples of five pieces. Since each piece is either defective or non-defective, the data are attribute data. A p-chart or np-chart can therefore be justified, depending on how you structure the analysis.

For Process 2, you must explain that the process records how many acceptable pieces are placed before a defect is found. This supports a g-chart or geometric-type interpretation, as seen in the stronger 2025 examples .

### 5. Compare Process 1 and Process 2 using the story of the data

The lecturer asks: what would you do if you had a dataset? You would look at the mean, variation, defects, capability, and process behaviour.

That means your report should compare the two processes using:

Defect counts
Defect proportions
Number of completed tangrams or figurines
Throughput
DPMO
Sigma level
Control chart behaviour
Cost or profitability effect
Practical process limitations
Final recommendation

The 2026 brief specifically requires you to determine whether enough tangrams can be assembled as figurines, identify defective pieces efficiently, and determine whether the process can run at a 2-sigma level in terms of defective parts per million .

## What this means for your report structure

Use this structure:

1. **Background**
   Introduce Tangra Inc., customer complaints, defective pieces, quality risk, and why the investigation is necessary.

2. **Problem Statement**
   State that defective pieces affect figurine assembly, customer satisfaction, throughput, and the ability to meet the 2-sigma requirement.

3. **Aim and Objectives**
   State that the aim is to compare Process 1 and Process 2 and recommend the better quality assurance approach.

4. **Literature Review**
   Discuss quality management, SPC, sampling inspection, process capability, DPMO, sigma level, and cost of quality.

5. **Methodology and Data Collection**
   Explain the Learning Factory experiment, Process 1 and Process 2 steps, sample size, recorded variables, time limit, assumptions, and limitations.

6. **Data Analysis**
   Include descriptive statistics, defect proportions, charts, p-chart or np-chart, g-chart, DPMO, sigma level, cost analysis, and process comparison.

7. **Discussion**
   Interpret what the results mean. Do not only describe charts. Explain which process is more stable, more capable, more efficient, and more financially suitable.

8. **Conclusion and Recommendation**
   Give a clear answer on which process Tangra Inc. should use and why.

9. **References**
   Use NWU Harvard style. The NWU guide states that sources must be acknowledged through in-text references and a complete reference list to avoid plagiarism .

## Strong warning from the lecturer’s memo

Do **not** submit a report that only contains calculations. The lecturer wants to see that you can:

Conduct an investigation
Use literature
Choose appropriate tools
Analyse data
Interpret results
Compare alternatives
Reach a justified conclusion

This is exactly what GA 4 requires in the assignment brief .
